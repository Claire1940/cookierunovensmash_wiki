## Gameplay Modes and Objectives

The core of *Cookie Run: OvenSmash* revolves around 3v3 team-based combat, though several experimental modes have been introduced to keep the gameplay fresh. Unlike the side-scrolling runners of the past, this is a top-down action game where positioning and ability timing are everything.

### Wild Royale (Battle Royale)
In this "every Cookie for themselves" mode, players drop into a shrinking arena. You must break crates to find Power Jellies, which increase your health and attack power. The goal is simple: be the last Cookie standing. Periodic stage hazards like rising lava or crumbling platforms add an extra layer of danger.

### Smash Arena
This is the standard 3v3 team deathmatch. The first team to reach a specific knockout count—usually 10 or 15—wins the match. This mode is ideal for learning the combat rhythms of new characters like Bacon Roll Cookie or Melon Soda Cookie.

### Gold Rush
A strategic looting mode where teams fight over gold bars. Each player starts with a gold bar, and knocking out an opponent allows you to steal theirs. If a team holds 10 or more gold bars for 15 seconds, they win instantly. If time runs out, the team with the most gold bars takes the trophy.

### Castle Crasher
A unique take on the "escort" genre. Teams must stay near a water cannon turtle (guided by a Sugar Gnome) to push it toward the enemy's sandcastle. The opposing team must stand near the turtle to halt its progress or push it back.

> **💡 Pro Tip:** In Gold Rush, don't hoard all the gold on one player. Spread the bars across the team to prevent a single knockout from resetting your win countdown.

## Customization and the Summon Shop

Progression in *OvenSmash* differs significantly from *Kingdom*. The "Gacha" system has been rebranded as the **Summon Shop**. Here, players use Crystals or General Summon Tickets to pull for Cookies and Soulchips.

1.  **Soulchips:** These are used to level up your Cookies, increasing their base health and damage output.
2.  **Passives:** Each Cookie can equip two passive effects. These can range from faster cooldowns to health regeneration when picking up Jellies.
3.  **Attack Variants:** You can choose between two different versions of your basic and special attacks, allowing you to tailor a Cookie to your playstyle (e.g., more range vs. more damage).

> **⚠️ Warning:** Always check the "Summon Rates" in the shop before spending your hard-earned Crystals. Epic Cookies like Red Velvet or Madeleine have significantly lower drop rates than Common Cookies.
