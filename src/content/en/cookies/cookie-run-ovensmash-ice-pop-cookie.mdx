## Skill Breakdown and Mechanics

The kit of the **cookie run ovensmash ice pop cookie** is designed around the "Freeze" mechanic. Freezing an enemy completely disables their movement and ability usage for a short duration, making it one of the most powerful forms of crowd control (CC) in the game.

### Basic Attack: Power Swing
Ice Pop Cookie swings his bat in a cone in front of him. This attack can be charged to increase its range significantly. At base, it has a range of 2.7, but a fully charged swing reaches up to 9.0, allowing you to poke enemies from a safe distance.

### Special Skill: Icy Sliding
This is your primary mobility tool. Ice Pop Cookie dashes in a chosen direction, dealing damage to anyone he passes through. Depending on your build, this skill can also provide defensive utility or additional movement debuffs.

### Ultimate: Freezing Bat Flip
This is the "game-changer" ability. Ice Pop Cookie throws or swings his bat with immense force, dealing massive damage (952 at base) and freezing all enemies hit for 1.5 seconds. In a team fight, hitting three or more enemies with this can result in an instant win.

| Skill Type | Skill Name | Effect | Cooldown |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Basic** | Power Swing | Cone damage; range increases with charge (up to 9.0). | 0.8s |
| **Special** | Icy Sliding | Dash forward dealing **536 damage** to enemies. | 10.0s |
| **Ultimate** | Freezing Bat Flip | Massive cone damage + **1.5s Freeze** effect. | 30.0s |

> **💡 Tip:** Always try to charge your Power Swing while hidden in bushes. The extended 9.0 range can catch enemies off-guard, initiating a fight before they even see you.

## Advanced Gameplay Strategies

To truly master the **cookie run ovensmash ice pop cookie**, you must move away from a "button-mashing" playstyle. Because his cooldowns (especially the 30-second Ultimate) are quite long, every ability must count.

### 1. The Hit-and-Run Loop
The most effective way to play Ice Pop Cookie is to use **Icy Sliding** to enter a fight, immediately trigger your **Power Swing** (charged if possible), and then back off. If the enemy tries to retaliate, your Icy Guard shield should absorb the initial damage while you retreat to your teammates.

### 2. Positioning in Team Fights
You are not the frontline. You should stand slightly behind your Tank but in front of your Marksman (like Rye Cookie). Your job is to watch for "dive" attempts. When an enemy tries to reach your backline, use **Freezing Bat Flip** to stop them. According to the [official Cookie Run website](https://www.cookierun.com), team synergy is the key to victory in OvenSmash's 30-player modes.

### 3. Objective Control
Ice Pop Cookie is a monster at securing objectives. When a boss or a chest is about to break, use your freeze to lock out the enemy team. This prevents them from using "Smash" abilities to steal the last hit.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not use your Ultimate purely for damage. The 1.5-second freeze is far more valuable than the 952 damage. Save it for when you can interrupt an enemy's Ultimate!

**Q: Is Ice Pop Cookie better than other Controllers in OvenSmash?**

A: In the 2026 meta, the **cookie run ovensmash ice pop cookie** is considered a top-tier Controller because of his "Freeze" status effect. While other cookies might have better slows, "Freeze" is a hard stun that is much harder to counter.

**Q: Can Ice Pop Cookie be played as a Main DPS?**

A: It is not recommended. While his Ultimate deals high damage, his basic attack speed is too slow to compete with dedicated damage dealers like Rye Cookie or Chili Pepper Cookie. He is much more effective when played as a utility-focused disruptor.

**Q: How do I counter an enemy Ice Pop Cookie?**

A: Look for cookies with "Unstoppable" or "Cleanse" mechanics. Additionally, because his dash has a 10-second cooldown, he is very vulnerable once he has used his mobility. Bait out the dash, then collapse on him before his shield or freeze is available again.
